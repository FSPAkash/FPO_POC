# Data and Effort Required

## Purpose
This document separates what we need for the POC from what we will need after approval for viable real-world deployment.

The key principle is:

- POC stage: use realistic synthetic data and simulated external signals.
- Post-approval stage: replace synthetic data with viable partner, public, field, and transactional data sources.

## Data Strategy
### POC now
Use synthetic data for every module, but ensure it is internally consistent:

- farmers belong to real-looking FPOs and villages
- plots belong to farmers
- crop seasons belong to plots
- demand aligns with crop and season
- procurement aligns with aggregated demand
- inventory aligns with purchase and issue records
- sales align with collected produce and buyer demand
- carbon practices align with crop, area, and geography

### Later viable data
After approval, we should acquire the minimum viable real data required to make the workflows credible in the field:

- partner FPO registry and member data
- field officer onboarding data
- actual crop season records
- real input catalogs and suppliers
- actual collection and settlement data
- mandi and buyer price feeds
- buyer and processor directories
- practice and plot data for climate readiness

## Data Domains
| Domain | Purpose in POC | Core fields | Synthetic now | Viable source later |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Geography | Hierarchy and filtering | state, district, block, village | Generated reference tables | Census, state agriculture directories, partner FPO mapping |
| FPO master | Organizational entity | FPO name, crop focus, members, warehouse capacity | 8 realistic FPO profiles | Partner FPO onboarding forms, registration docs |
| Farmer master | Member registry | farmer id, village, contact, land size, irrigation, soil | 1,200-2,000 members | FPO member ledgers, field onboarding |
| Plot data | Land intelligence | farmer id, area, boundary, irrigation source, soil | GeoJSON polygons and area stats | GPS survey, cadastral or consented field mapping |
| Crop seasons | Agronomy context | crop, sowing date, expected harvest, variety | 2 seasons across key crops | FPO records, field staff updates, crop surveys |
| Communication profiles | Channel preferences | phone, language, preferred mode | Generated by region/language | farmer onboarding and consent capture |
| Advisory content | Message and recommendation templates | crop, stage, advisory type, content | curated template library | agronomy experts, package of practices |
| Message logs | Communication evidence | intent, message type, status, escalation | simulated conversations | WhatsApp or app logs |
| Input catalog | Procurement base | item, category, unit, supplier | generated with price bands | supplier catalogs, FPO procurement records |
| Supplier master | Procurement counterparties | name, lead time, payment terms | 15-25 suppliers | supplier onboarding and contracts |
| Input demand | Procurement planning | farmer, item, qty, village, date | scenario-driven demand records | WhatsApp capture, field officer entry |
| Inventory | Stock visibility | item, warehouse, stock, reorder level | generated from transactions | warehouse records, GRN logs |
| Produce collection | Post-harvest workflow | farmer, crop, grade, quantity, center | synthetic lot records | collection center entries, weighment slips |
| Buyer master | Market network | buyer type, location, crop demand, terms | 25-40 buyers | buyer discovery, partner onboarding |
| Market prices | Price intelligence | crop, mandi, min, max, avg, date | synthetic daily time series | eNAM, APMC, market portals |
| Buyer demand | Deal flow | crop, quantity, date, location, price | generated purchase demand | direct buyer submissions, sales team logs |
| Sales history | Analytics and matching | crop, buyer, qty, price, payment status | synthetic completed orders | historical FPO sales records |
| Carbon practices | Climate activity log | practice, area, date, duration | generated by crop and region | field surveys, farmer reporting, partner programs |
| Satellite indicators | Climate verification proxy | NDVI, soil moisture, cover indicator | simulated indices | Sentinel or partner remote sensing pipeline |
| Carbon factors | Estimation engine | practice factor, crop coefficient | rules table | scientific methodology and verifier-approved factors |

## Minimum Synthetic Dataset for POC
### Foundation dataset
- 2 states
- 4 districts
- 10-15 villages per district cluster
- 8 FPOs
- 1,500 farmers
- 3,000 plots
- 4 main crops plus 1 horticulture crop
- 2 seasons

### Operations dataset
- 700 input demand records
- 150 purchase requests
- 120 purchase orders
- 120 goods receipts
- 500 inventory movements
- 450 produce collection entries
- 200 farmer settlement records

### Market dataset
- 180 days of mandi prices for 5 crops across 6 markets
- 30 buyers
- 80 buyer demand postings
- 60 sales orders
- 60 dispatches

### Communication dataset
- 1,000 message logs
- 150 advisory logs
- 80 image or disease case logs
- 50 escalations
- 40 broadcast campaigns

### Carbon dataset
- 700 practice records
- 700 carbon estimate rows
- 12 aggregated carbon projects

## Scenario Design Requirements
The synthetic data must tell believable stories. At minimum we need these scenarios:

- Normal seasonal demand aggregation for fertilizer and seed.
- Produce collection surge during harvest.
- Inventory shortage and reorder.
- Better buyer offer than mandi benchmark.
- Delayed payment and settlement visibility.
- Disease query escalation due to low confidence.
- Carbon project aggregation from many small farmers.

## Effort Estimate for POC Build
### Assumed team
- 1 full-stack lead
- 1 frontend-focused engineer
- 1 backend/data engineer
- 1 product and business analyst part-time
- 1 QA/design support part-time

### Indicative duration
6 to 8 weeks for a usable POC if scope is controlled.

### Workstream effort
| Workstream | Main output | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery and product framing | workflow definition, data model, screen map | 4-5 days |
| App scaffolding and architecture | React and Flask base, auth shell, DB setup | 4-5 days |
| Synthetic data design and seeding | generators, scenario logic, reset scripts | 5-7 days |
| Registry module | FPO, farmer, plot, season screens and APIs | 5-6 days |
| Operations module | demand, procurement, inventory, collection, settlement | 8-10 days |
| Market linkage module | prices, buyers, matching, sales orders | 5-6 days |
| Communication simulator | chat UI, logs, advisory, escalation | 4-5 days |
| Carbon readiness module | practice tracking, estimates, aggregation | 4-5 days |
| Dashboards and reports | summary views and KPIs | 3-4 days |
| QA, polish, and demo prep | fixes, seeded stories, walkthrough | 4-5 days |

### Practical view
If we must reduce scope, keep these first:

- registry
- operations
- market linkage

Then add:

- communication simulator
- carbon readiness

## Effort Estimate After POC Approval
The real work starts after approval because viable data acquisition is operationally heavier than UI development.

### Post-approval workstreams
| Workstream | What changes after POC | Indicative effort |
|---|---|---|
| FPO partner onboarding | identify pilot FPOs, align scope, sign-off | 2-3 weeks |
| Real data mapping | map existing ledgers, Excel files, and formats | 2-4 weeks |
| Field data collection design | forms, SOPs, validation, staff training | 2-3 weeks |
| Master data cleansing | dedupe members, villages, plots, crops | 2-4 weeks |
| External data integration | mandi, weather, buyer, GIS feeds | 2-5 weeks |
| Workflow adaptation | fit software to actual FPO operations | 2-4 weeks |
| Pilot support and iteration | live corrections and adoption support | 4-8 weeks |

This stage will likely take longer than the POC build itself.

## Data Acquisition Plan After Approval
### Priority 1: operationally critical data
- FPO profile and governance structure
- member registry
- village mapping
- crop and season records
- input catalog and suppliers
- produce collection formats
- payment and settlement process

### Priority 2: market linkage data
- market price sources
- buyer directory
- historical FPO sales
- payment reliability and logistics constraints

### Priority 3: climate and land intelligence data
- plot boundaries
- climate practice records
- soil and irrigation attributes
- satellite-ready identifiers
- approved carbon methodology assumptions

## Risks in Data Work
- Synthetic data may look random if not scenario-based.
- Real FPO data may be incomplete, duplicated, or politically sensitive.
- Plot boundary data may be unavailable or inconsistent.
- Historical sales and payment records may exist only in paper or spreadsheet form.
- Carbon-readiness data may require consent and stronger evidence standards.

## Quality Rules for Synthetic Data
- Every transaction must reconcile with a prior state.
- Crop, season, and geography must stay internally consistent.
- Financial values should be within realistic local ranges.
- Inventory should never go negative unless explicitly modeling an exception.
- Buyer demand and price trends should create visible decision tradeoffs.
- Carbon estimates should be conservative and formula-based.

## Recommended Deliverables Alongside the POC
- data dictionary
- entity relationship diagram
- seed generation scripts
- scenario catalog for demos
- API contract document
- pilot data acquisition checklist

## Recommendation
Treat data as a first-class workstream, not a side task. The quality of the POC will depend less on visual polish and more on whether the synthetic data behaves like a believable FPO operating environment.

If we execute that well, approval conversations become easier because stakeholders can clearly see:

- what the platform does
- what data each module needs
- which data is mocked now
- what must be collected or integrated later
